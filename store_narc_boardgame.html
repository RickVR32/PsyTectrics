The board

Five rings.
One centre.

All players start on Ring 1 — the outer Reactive Zone. The goal is to move inward ring by ring toward Zero-Point at the centre. Only SIST responses move you inward. NARC and YARC accumulate Drift.

Rings 1–2 — Reactive Zone

Where all players start. High Trigger and Escalation space density. Fast moving but Drift accumulates quickly here.

Rings 3–4 — Pattern-Aware Zone

Trigger and ØLL spaces increase. Observer spaces appear. You can see the loop from here — but it still pulls.

Ring 5 — SIST Proximity

Mostly SIST and ØLL spaces. ØLL events hit hardest here. So close — but this is where the loop makes its last play.

Zero-Point — Win Condition

Cannot be reached by NARC or YARC moves — only SIST inward movement earns it. First player to reach Zero-Point wins.

What makes it different

The mechanics.
The framework in play.

🎲

Roll and Move

Roll both dice and move along your ring. Land on Trigger, Drift, Response, Observer or Escalation spaces. Each one activates a different mechanic.

📈

The Drift System

Every NARC and YARC response adds a Drift token. At 5 tokens — you're forced back a full ring. Drift is always visible to all players. The board doesn't lie.

◆

SIST Moves You Inward

The only way to reach the next ring inward is through a SIST response. SIST is always slower. Always harder. And always the only path that compounds without cost.

👁

The Witness Effect

If 3+ players are watching your turn, you must choose your response card face-up before the effect is revealed. Awareness rises when witnessed. The game makes it mechanical.

🔄

The Loop Trap

A NARC and a YARC played consecutively on the same Trigger — both players draw an ØLL Event card. Neither moves forward. This is the loop. It's unavoidable if both keep playing the same codes.

📊

Observer Code Readout

At game end every player gets their Observer Code from their Player Board ratio. The win condition is secondary to the self-awareness the game surfaces.

The Drift System

The hidden cost.

Drift is the game's core tension mechanic. Every NARC and YARC card adds a token. All tokens are visible to all players. At 5 — you don't choose to go back. The board sends you back.
